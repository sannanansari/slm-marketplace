SLM vs LLM

A clear breakdown of what makes Small Language Models different from Large Language Models — size, cost, speed, privacy, and when each is the right choice.

The Core Difference

SLMs (1B–7B parameters) and LLMs (70B–700B+) are the same architecture — transformers. The difference is scale and what that scale buys you.

PropertySLM (1B–7B)LLM (70B+)
Parameters1B–7B70B–700B+
VRAM needed2–8 GB40 GB–1 TB+
Runs on laptop?✅ Yes❌ No
Fine-tuning cost$0–$50 (Colab)$10,000+
Inference costFree (local)$0.001–$0.06/1K tokens
Private/offline?✅ Yes❌ Cloud required
Domain expertiseExcellent (after fine-tuning)Good (prompt only)
General reasoningGoodExcellent

When to Choose Each

Choose SLM when: you need privacy, offline capability, domain specialisation, low latency, or zero API cost. Healthcare, legal, finance, on-device AI.
Choose LLM when: you need broad general knowledge, complex multi-step reasoning across many domains, or you're building a general-purpose assistant.

Real-World Example

A law firm needs to classify 10,000 contract clauses daily. Using GPT-4 at $0.03/1K tokens with avg 500 tokens/clause = $150/day = $54,750/year. A fine-tuned 1B SLM runs on a $500 server, costs $0/query, and achieves higher accuracy on legal text. ROI: positive in month 1.
