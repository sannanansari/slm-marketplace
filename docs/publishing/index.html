📤 Publishing Guide

Publish Your SLM to the World

A model nobody can find helps nobody. This guide covers the complete publishing workflow: writing a compelling model card, choosing a license, uploading to HuggingFace Hub, and listing on SLM Marketplace so your work reaches the people who need it.

⏱ 20 min read 🎯 Beginner-friendly 🏆 Build your reputation

Why Publishing Matters

Every model you publish does three things simultaneously:

  1. Helps other engineers who need the same domain capability
  2. Builds your reputation as a practitioner — downloads are a real signal
  3. Forces rigor — writing a model card makes you actually evaluate your model properly
The SLM Marketplace difference: HuggingFace has 800K+ models. It's hard to get discovered. SLM Marketplace is domain-specific — smaller, higher-intent audience, purpose-built for exactly what you've built.

Before You Publish — Checklist

Run through this before pushing to Hub:
  • ✅ Model evaluated on at least 50 held-out test examples
  • ✅ Eval metrics documented (perplexity, accuracy, ROUGE, or domain-specific)
  • ✅ 10+ qualitative examples showing real model outputs
  • ✅ Compared to base model on same tasks
  • ✅ Tested for obvious failure modes / harmful outputs
  • ✅ License chosen and reviewed
  • ✅ Training dataset card exists (or dataset is documented)
  • ✅ GGUF quantized version ready
  • ✅ Model card written (template below)

Choosing a License

LicenseCommercial useDerivative worksShare-alikeBest for
MIT ✅ Yes ✅ Yes ❌ No Maximum openness. Most popular for small models. Phi-3 uses MIT.
Apache 2.0 ✅ Yes ✅ Yes ❌ No Like MIT but includes patent grant. Gemma, Qwen, Mistral use Apache 2.0.
CC BY 4.0 ✅ Yes ✅ Yes (with credit) ❌ No Good for research models. Attribution required.
CC BY-SA 4.0 ✅ Yes ✅ Yes ✅ Yes Copyleft — derivatives must use same license.
CC BY-NC 4.0 ❌ No ✅ Yes ❌ No Research only. No commercial use by anyone.
Llama 3.2 Community ⚠️ Conditional ⚠️ Conditional — Fine-tunes of Llama 3.2 must use this license (Meta requires it).
Critical: If your model is fine-tuned from Llama, Gemma, or Phi-3, your model's license must be at least as restrictive as the base model's license. You cannot use MIT if the base model is Llama 3.2 (which requires the Llama Community License). Always check the base model's license first.

Writing Your Model Card

A model card is your README. It's the first thing every potential user reads. A bad model card means zero downloads. Here is a production-quality template:

markdown
---
license: apache-2.0
language:
- en
tags:
- llama
- legal
- contract-analysis
- fine-tuned
- qlora
base_model: meta-llama/Llama-3.2-1B-Instruct
datasets:
- your-username/legal-contracts-dataset
pipeline_tag: text-generation
---

# LegalEagle-1B — Contract Analysis SLM

A 1B parameter SLM fine-tuned on 1,200 legal contract examples for
clause identification, risk assessment, and plain-English explanation.

## Quick Start

```python
from transformers import AutoTokenizer, AutoModelForCausalLM
import torch

model = AutoModelForCausalLM.from_pretrained(
    "your-username/legaleagle-1b",
    torch_dtype=torch.float16,
    device_map="auto"
)
tokenizer = AutoTokenizer.from_pretrained("your-username/legaleagle-1b")

messages = [
    {"role": "system", "content": "You are a legal contract analyst."},
    {"role": "user", "content": "Analyze: 'Either party may terminate with 30 days notice.'"}
]
text = tokenizer.apply_chat_template(messages, tokenize=False, add_generation_prompt=True)
inputs = tokenizer(text, return_tensors="pt").to(model.device)
outputs = model.generate(**inputs, max_new_tokens=300, temperature=0.3)
print(tokenizer.decode(outputs[0], skip_special_tokens=True))
```

## Training Data

Fine-tuned on [legal-contracts-dataset](https://huggingface.co/datasets/your-username/legal-contracts-dataset):
- 900 training examples (annotated contract clauses with expert analysis)
- 150 validation examples
- 150 test examples
- Sources: public domain contracts, Creative Commons legal texts, synthetic examples

## Intended Use

✅ **Suitable for:**
- Legal document pre-screening
- Contract clause identification and categorization
- Plain-English explanation of legal terms
- First-pass risk flagging before attorney review
- Legal education and training tools

❌ **Not suitable for:**
- Replacing licensed legal counsel
- Binding legal interpretation
- Jurisdictions outside common law systems
- Criminal or family law matters

## Limitations

- Trained primarily on U.S. and U.K. commercial contracts
- May not recognize jurisdiction-specific provisions (EU, APAC)
- Does not understand context beyond the provided clause text
- Performance degrades on highly technical IP or financial contracts

Upload to SLM Marketplace

SLM Marketplace is purpose-built for SLMs. Listing here gives you exposure to engineers specifically looking for small, specialized models.

  1. Create an account at slm-market.sannan.app/auth
  2. Click "Upload Model" in the navigation bar
  3. Fill in the required fields:
    • Model name (must be unique)
    • Description (markdown supported — be detailed)
    • Category (text-generation, classification, embedding, etc.)
    • Tags (your domain, base model, technique)
    • HuggingFace URL (link to your Hub model)
    • License
    • Parameter count
  4. Add benchmark results — models with eval scores get significantly more visibility
  5. Submit — your model appears immediately
Pro tip: Models with all fields filled, benchmark scores, and a detailed description get 5–10× more views than bare-minimum listings. Spend 20 minutes on the description — it pays off.

Building an Audience

Publishing is step one. Here's how to get your model discovered:

✍️
Write a Technical Blog Post
Document your process on Medium, Substack, or dev.to. Include your eval results. Link to your HuggingFace and SLM Marketplace pages. Engineers who find useful write-ups bookmark and share them.
🐦
Share on Social Media
Post on Twitter/X and LinkedIn with your benchmark results. Tag @huggingface. The AI community on Twitter is extremely active and shares good work generously.
💬
Post in Communities
r/MachineLearning, r/LocalLLaMA, HuggingFace Discord, and domain-specific communities (r/legaltech, r/healthcareit). Share results, not just links — show what it can do.
🎮
Create a Live Demo
HuggingFace Spaces (free Gradio demo) lets users try your model in the browser without downloading anything. A live demo converts viewers to users far better than a static README.

Common Publishing Mistakes

  • Publishing without evaluation — "Works on my machine" isn't documentation. Run the eval, write the numbers.
  • Wrong license — Publishing a Llama fine-tune under MIT violates Meta's license. Always check your base model's license first.
  • No GGUF version — Most users want to run locally with Ollama or LM Studio. No GGUF = fewer downloads.
  • Vague model name — "my-model-v2" tells nobody anything. "legaleagle-1b-contract-analysis" is findable.
  • Missing base_model tag — HuggingFace indexes this for discovery. Always set base_model: in your card front matter.
  • No example outputs — The single highest-impact addition to any model card. Show what it does, not just what it is.
